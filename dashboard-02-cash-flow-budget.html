9. Critical Alerts
🚨 Alert 1: Credit Card Interest Is a Financial Emergency
CC interest grew from $345 (2023) → $2,523 (2024) → $12,559 (2025). At current balances across three cards (~$43,000+), projected 2026 interest is $14,000–$15,000 — more than $1,200/month in pure waste. The budget targets $0 CC interest, but that requires paying off all balances first.

Card breakdown: Citi AA ~$28,367 @ 27.24% APR ($7,726/yr) · Citi DC ~$14,403 @ 24.24% APR ($3,490/yr) · Capital One est. ~$12,000 @ 25% (~$3,000/yr)
⚠️ Alert 2: Chase ACH Payments Are Unaccounted For in 2024 and 2025
The 2023 summary confirmed Chase ACH = second mortgage ($39,417 that year). In 2024 (~$53,281) and 2025 (~$41,000 est.), these payments were excluded from the spending summaries. The true total cash outflow in 2025 is approximately $138,177 + $41,000 + $29,700 taxes = ~$208,877.
⚠️ Alert 3: Tax Payments Spiked to $29,700 in 2025
IRS and Illinois payments jumped from $2,633 (2024) to $29,700 (2025) — a $27,067 increase. This suggests significant under-withholding, a large freelance/self-employment income event, a capital gain, or prior-year adjustments. This needs to be understood before finalizing the 2026 budget.
📋 Alert 4: Budget Needs a Major Rewrite
The current budget underestimates several fixed costs by large margins:
Health insurance: $120/mo budgeted vs. $953/mo actual (+694%) · Home insurance: $53/mo vs. $228/mo actual (+330%) · Phone: $200/mo vs. $350/mo actual (now fixed via T-Mobile) · Gas/heating: $150/mo vs. $262/mo actual (+75%) · Groceries: $350/mo vs. $920/mo actual (+163%)

A realistic revised budget for living expenses is ~$7,500–$8,000/month, leaving approximately $1,400/month disposable — before CC debt paydown.
