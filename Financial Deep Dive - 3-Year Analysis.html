Tax Optimization Opportunities
Your tax situation has unique opportunities, especially with self-employment and rental income
What You're Doing Right
Qualified Business Income Deduction (QBI): In 2025, your self-employment income qualified for a $16,180 QBI deduction (20% of qualified income). This saved you ~$4,000 in taxes.
Rental Depreciation: Your $29-31K/year in depreciation creates a paper loss that shelters rental income from taxes. You have $19,335 in suspended passive losses that can offset future gains.
HSA Contributions: In 2025, you contributed the max $4,300 to your HSA and deducted self-employed health insurance ($11,561). Both reduce taxable income.
Self-employed health insurance deduction: The $11,561 deduction is an above-the-line deduction that reduces AGI directly.
Opportunities You're Missing
Solo 401k / SEP-IRA: If you're self-employed, you can contribute up to $69,000/year (2025) to a Solo 401k — far more than an employee 401k. On $100K net SE income, you could shelter ~$23K (employee) + ~$18.5K (employer) = $41,500. This could save $10,000+ in taxes.
Estimated tax management: You overpaid $12,260 in federal estimated taxes for 2025. That's a free loan to the IRS. Dial back estimated payments and invest the difference.
Roth conversions: If 2025's lower AGI ($133K) continues, you're in a relatively lower bracket. Consider converting some Traditional IRA to Roth now — pay taxes at today's rate to avoid higher rates in retirement or when RMDs start.
529 state tax deduction: Illinois allows up to $10K/year in 529 deductions. At the 4.95% state rate, that's $495 in free money you're leaving on the table.
